Research Activities

My research interests are around cryptologic problems with a focus on Post-Quantum Cryptography, i.e. quantum-resistant cryptography.
  • I held in Wollongong with the First Workshop on Mathematics and Algorithms for Cryptographic Advanced Objects
  • I deal with lattice theory for cryptography, a recent example is my participation to NIST Call for Post-Quantum Cryptography Standardization where I submited two candidates for Round 1:
    • DRS, A lattice based signature
    • Odd Manhattan, A lattice based Key Encapsulation Mechanism
  • I helped build an Ideal Lattice Challenge.
  • Using lattice, I also did multiple works on Homomorphic Encryption, i.e. encyption of data which allows its manipulation without decrypting it.
  • Since my Ph.D., I also worked on modular arithmetic and number system with a focus on building efficient and side-channel resistant cryptosystems, in particular for Elliptic Curve Cryptography.
My last work on
  • Modular Arithmetic is Efficient Word Size Modular Arithmetic, accepted to be published at IEEE Transactions on Emerging Topics in Computing. (paper.pdf)
